WCAG 2.2 Quick Guide – Introduction

Center for Teaching and Research Excellence · Chicago State University

Welcome to the WCAG 2.2 Faculty Quick Guide

This interactive toolkit is designed to help CSU faculty quickly understand and apply the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 in courses, syllabi, Brightspace pages, and instructional materials.

WCAG 2.2 is built on four principles — content must be Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust. The CTRE has expanded this into five modules to help you apply accessibility to real teaching practices.

Explore each module description below for clear explanations and practical steps you can use immediately.

1

Module 1: Perceivable

Ensuring students can see, hear, or otherwise access the information you present. Includes alt text, captions, document structure, and color contrast.

2

Module 2: Operable

Making sure students can navigate and operate your course with different tools, such as keyboard-only navigation, clear links, adequate timing, and meaningful feedback.

3

Module 3: Understandable

Content and instructions must be written clearly, behave consistently, and support students in avoiding or correcting mistakes in quizzes, forms, and assignments.

4

Module 4: Robust

Ensuring course materials work across different devices, browsers, and assistive technologies. Focuses on clean structure, meaningful labels, and clear status messages.

5

Module 5: Applying WCAG in Your Course

A practical, teaching-focused guide that helps you apply accessibility throughout the semester — including checklists, weekly habits, and common quick fixes.
